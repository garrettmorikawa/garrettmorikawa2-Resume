Hi, my name is Garrett Morikawa and I am currently a freshman at the University of California Riverside. I am in the pre-business program, and am planning on going to the University of California Riverside's Business School. I grew up in a small town in Los Angeles called La Canada. I went to La Canada High School, where I also played soccer. Some of my interests outside of the classroom is soccer and cooking or eating. I played soccer for a club team and for my high school team. Soccer was a big part of my life growing up, and it allowed me to be social, meet new people, and be competitive in that nature. Growing up in Southern California, the wide expanse of cultures and food amazed me. During my time in high school, I had a few jobs throughout the years, and had experienced many different positions. My first job was at Taylor's Steakhouse in my hometown where I was a bus boy, cleaning tables and washing dishes. For it being my first job, it wasn't a horrible experience, as I learned the ins and outs of how the restaurant works. I was very close with the manager of the establishment and he kind of took me under his wing and taught me a lot about the business aspect of the restaurant. Even though I saw him have a stressed look in his face everyday, the ability to keep up with demand was impressive. My second job was actually working under my parents. My parents own and operate their own business in Los Angeles. They control a linen business that supplies some big restaurants in the Los Angeles area. Working for my parents taught me the most about how to run a business, which is what I would want to do someday. Overall, my journey through the ranks of a business would help me further my education into this field.
